The History of Pluto

Clyde Tombaugh

In 1906, Percival Lowell (founder of the Lowell Observatory) started a search for "Planet X", a possible ninth planet in our solar system. Despite capturing two images of Pluto, Lowell failed to recognize the object for what it was (foreshadowing). After Lowell's passing, the search for Planet X was resumed in 1929. On February 18, 1930, Pluto was officially discovered by 23-year-old Clyde Tombaugh (pog). It was named after the Roman god of the underworld, as suggested by 11-year-old Venetia Burney (even more pog).

Later that year, the name "Pluto" was adopted by Walt Disney for Mickey Mouse's pet dog. About a decade later, chemist Glenn Seaborg chose the name "Plutonium" for the newly-created element.
